Dindigul Thalapakatti Mutton Biryani

Dindigul Thalapakatti Mutton Biryani is a famous South Indian biryani known for its unique flavor, made with small-grain seeraga samba rice and a special blend of spices. Here's an authentic recipe to recreate this delicious dish at home.

Ingredients:

For Marination:
  • 500g mutton (cut into medium-sized pieces)
  • 1/2 cup curd/yogurt.
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste.
  • 1 tbsp red chili powder.
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder.
  • 1 tsp coriander powder.
  • 1 tsp fennel powder.
  • 1 tsp black pepper powder.
  • 1 tsp salt.
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice.

For Biryani:
  • 2 cups seeraga samba rice.
  • 4 tbsp ghee (or oil).
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced and 2 tomatoes, finely chopped, 2 green chilies, slit.
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint and coriander leaves, chopped.
  • 1 bay leaf, 1-inch cinnamon stick, 3 cloves and 2 cardamom pods.
  • 1 star anise, 1/2 tsp fennel seeds, 1/2 tsp cumin seeds, and 1/2 tsp black peppercorns.
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder, 4 cups water and Salt to taste.

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Mutton:
    In a large bowl, combine the mutton pieces with curd, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, fennel powder, black pepper powder, salt, and lemon juice. Mix well to coat the mutton evenly with the spices. Cover and let it marinate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight in the refrigerator for enhanced flavor.

  2. Prepare the Rice:
    Wash the seeraga samba rice thoroughly in water until it runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 20 minutes, then drain and set aside.

  3. Cook the Mutton:
    Heat ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan or pressure cooker over medium heat. Add bay leaf, cinnamon stick, cloves, cardamom, star anise, fennel seeds, cumin seeds, and black peppercorns. Saut√© until the spices release their aroma. Add the sliced onions and cook until they turn golden brown. Add the slit green chilies and chopped tomatoes, and cook until the tomatoes become soft and mushy. Add the marinated mutton to the pan. Cook until the mutton browns slightly and releases its juices. Add the chopped mint and coriander leaves. Stir well and cook for a few minutes. Sprinkle garam masala powder over the mutton mixture and mix thoroughly. Pour in 4 cups of water and add salt to taste. Stir well.

  4. Cook the Biryani:
    If using a pressure cooker, close the lid and cook for 3-4 whistles on medium heat, or until the mutton is tender. If using a regular pot, cover and cook on medium heat until the mutton is tender, about 25-30 minutes. Once the mutton is cooked, add the soaked and drained seeraga samba rice to the mutton mixture. Mix gently to combine. Cover with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, until the rice is fully cooked and the water is absorbed.

  5. Final Steps:
    Once the rice is cooked, remove the biryani from heat and let it rest for 10 minutes, covered. Gently fluff the biryani with a fork before serving, ensuring the rice grains remain separate.

  6. Serving Suggestions:
    Serve the Dindigul Thalapakatti Mutton Biryani hot with onion raita, boiled eggs, and pickle. Enjoy the rich and aromatic flavors of this iconic Dindigul Thalapakatti Mutton Biryani!
